# How to Review a Pull Request

Use the **"I Like / I Wish / What If"** framework to give kind, precise, and actionable feedback.

---

## Step 1: Open the PR

- Go to the **Pull requests** tab on the repo
- Click on your partner's PR
- Click the **Files changed** tab to see what they changed

## Step 2: Leave Line Comments (at least 2)

Click the **+** icon next to a line to leave a comment.

### "I like..." — Specific praise

> **Example:**
> "I like that your commit message clearly describes what you added — it makes the history easy to read."

### "I wish..." — Specific improvement request

> **Example:**
> "I wish the 'One Thing I Want to Learn' column had a bit more detail — what specifically about Git interests you?"

## Step 3: Leave a Summary Comment (at least 1)

Go back to the **Conversation** tab and leave a review summary.

### "What if..." — A suggestion or alternative

> **Example:**
> "What if you also added your year/major? It could help people find study partners in the same program."

## Step 4: Submit Your Review

- Click **Review changes** (green button, top right of Files changed)
- Choose one:
  - **Approve** — everything looks good
  - **Request changes** — there's something that should be fixed first
- Click **Submit review**

---

## Tips for Great Reviews

- **Be kind** — the person put effort into this
- **Be specific** — "this is wrong" is not helpful; "this line could be clearer because..." is
- **Be actionable** — suggest what to do, not just what's wrong
- **Respond to reviews on your own PR** — say what you changed and why
